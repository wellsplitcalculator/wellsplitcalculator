About the Rent Split by Income Calculator

The WellSplit Rent Split by Income Calculator divides monthly rent according to each person's share of total household income.

Someone earning a larger share of the combined income pays a larger share of the rent. Someone earning less pays less. An optional minimum contribution can be used when the household wants everyone to pay at least a fixed amount.

How to use this calculator

  • Enter the total monthly rent and choose a currency.
  • Choose monthly take-home or gross income.
  • Add each person and enter their monthly income.
  • Optionally set a minimum payment per person.
  • Calculate each person's income share and rent amount.

This method is useful for couples, roommates, and households with different income levels. Everyone should agree on the income figures and final split.

Income-Based Rent Split FAQs

Quick answers about proportional rent splitting by income.

What is a rent split by income calculator?

It divides rent according to each person's share of total household income.

How does it work?

Enter the rent and each person's monthly income. The calculator assigns rent proportionally.

What income should I enter?

Use each person's monthly take-home income for the most practical result.

Can I use gross income?

Yes, but everyone should use the same income type.

How is each share calculated?

Each person's income percentage is applied to the total monthly rent.

Is income-based rent splitting fair?

It can be fair when incomes differ significantly and everyone agrees.

Can couples use this calculator?

Yes. Add each partner separately or combine them as one household group.

Can I add more than two people?

Yes. Add as many people as needed, up to the calculator limit.

What if someone has no income?

Their calculated share will be zero unless a minimum contribution is used.

What is a minimum contribution?

It sets the smallest rent amount any person must pay.

Does the minimum change other shares?

Yes. The remaining rent is redistributed among the other people.

What if everyone earns the same amount?

The rent will be split equally.

Should bonuses be included?

Only include them if they are regular and everyone agrees.

Can I use annual income?

Convert annual income to monthly income before entering it.

Does this include utilities?

No. It calculates rent only.

Can utilities also be split by income?

Yes, but calculate them separately.

Can I copy the result?

Yes. Use the Copy Result button.

Do all shares add up to the full rent?

Yes, apart from minor display rounding.

Can I choose a currency?

Yes. Select the currency symbol you need.

Is the result legally binding?

No. It is an estimate for household discussions.
