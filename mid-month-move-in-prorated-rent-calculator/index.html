About the Mid-Month Move-In Prorated Rent Calculator

This calculator estimates rent for tenants who move in after the first day of the month or occupy a rental for only part of a month.

It supports the actual-days method and the fixed 30-day method. You can also add a move-out date for a shorter partial-month stay.

How to use it

  • Enter the full monthly rent.
  • Select the move-in date.
  • Choose the lease's proration method.
  • Optionally add a move-out date.
  • Choose whether the move-out day is billed.
  • Calculate the daily rate and prorated amount.

Always compare the result with the lease because landlords may use a specific proration rule.

Prorated Rent Calculator FAQs

Helpful answers about mid-month rent calculations.

What is a prorated rent calculator?

It calculates rent for only the days a tenant occupies the rental during a partial month.

How does mid-month proration work?

The monthly rent is converted to a daily rate, then multiplied by the number of billable days.

What daily-rate method should I use?

Use the actual days in the month unless your lease requires a fixed 30-day method.

What is the actual-days method?

It divides monthly rent by the number of calendar days in that month.

What is the 30-day method?

It divides monthly rent by 30, regardless of the month's actual length.

Is the move-in day included?

Yes. This calculator includes the move-in date as a billable day.

Is the move-out day included?

You can choose whether the move-out day is included.

Can I calculate only a move-in proration?

Yes. Leave the move-out option off and the calculator will charge through the end of the month.

Can I calculate a partial stay within one month?

Yes. Enable the move-out date and enter the last day of occupancy.

Can the move-out date be before move-in?

No. The calculator will ask you to correct the dates.

Does this work for February?

Yes. The actual-days method automatically uses 28 or 29 days.

Can I use any currency?

Yes. Select the currency symbol you want displayed.

Does this include deposits?

No. It calculates prorated rent only.

Does this include utilities?

No. Utilities should be prorated separately if needed.

Can I add a fixed fee?

No. Add lease fees separately to keep the rent calculation clear.

How many days are charged?

The calculator shows the exact billable-day count in the result.

Do landlords always prorate rent?

Not always. Check the lease and local rental rules.

Can I copy the result?

Yes. Use the Copy Result button.

Is the result exact?

It is mathematically exact for the method selected, subject to currency rounding.

Is the result legally binding?

No. It is an estimate and should be checked against the lease.
