About the Unequal Room Rent Calculator

The WellSplit Unequal Room Rent Calculator helps roommates divide rent fairly when bedrooms differ in size, privacy, amenities, or occupancy. It is designed for shared apartments, houses, student rentals, and homes where an equal split would not reflect the actual value of each room.

The calculator combines an equal portion with a weighted portion. The equal portion is divided evenly among rooms, while the weighted portion uses room size, private bathroom access, balcony or patio access, parking, storage, extra occupants, and custom room adjustments.

How to use this unequal rent split calculator

  • Enter the total monthly rent and number of rooms.
  • Choose how much rent should be equal and how much should be unequal.
  • Select square feet or square metres for room measurements.
  • Set the feature importance sliders to match your household's priorities.
  • Add each room's size, occupants, amenities, and custom adjustment.
  • Calculate each room's total and the amount owed per person.

This tool is useful for roommates with unequal bedrooms, couples sharing a room, rooms with private bathrooms, parking differences, and other situations where a simple equal rent split may not feel fair. The result is a practical starting point for discussion, and all roommates should agree to the final arrangement.

Unequal Room Rent Calculator FAQs

These answers explain common ways to split rent by room size, amenities, occupancy, and shared household value.

What is an unequal room rent calculator?

An unequal room rent calculator helps roommates divide monthly rent when bedrooms differ in size, amenities, or occupancy. It gives each room a weighted share instead of forcing an equal split.

How does this unequal rent calculator work?

Enter the total rent, choose the equal and unequal portions, set the feature weights, and add each room's details. The calculator combines room size, amenities, occupants, and custom adjustments to calculate each room's total.

How should roommates split rent when rooms are different sizes?

A common method is to divide part of the rent equally and divide the rest according to room size and features. Larger or better-equipped rooms usually pay more than smaller rooms.

What does the equal portion of rent mean?

The equal portion is divided evenly among all rooms. It represents shared access to the home and keeps the final amounts from being based only on bedroom differences.

What does the unequal portion of rent mean?

The unequal portion is distributed according to room size, private features, extra occupants, and any custom adjustment selected for each room.

What percentage of rent should be divided equally?

There is no universal percentage. Many roommate households choose 30% to 50% as the equal portion, then use room differences for the remainder.

How much more should a larger bedroom pay?

The difference depends on the room-size gap and the percentage of rent assigned to the unequal portion. A larger unequal portion makes room size affect the result more strongly.

How much is a private bathroom worth in a rent split?

A private bathroom often justifies a higher room share because it provides privacy and exclusive use. The calculator lets you control the premium with the private bathroom weight.

Should a balcony or patio increase a room's rent?

Yes, when the balcony or patio is private or mainly benefits one room. The calculator includes an adjustable balcony or patio premium.

Should parking be included in a roommate rent split?

Parking can be included when one room receives a reserved or more valuable space. The parking weight lets the household decide how strongly it affects the split.

How should storage space affect rent?

A large closet or private storage area can increase a room's value. The calculator allows standard, above-average, or large private storage selections.

How do extra occupants affect the rent split?

Additional occupants can increase a room's share because more people use the home's common areas. You can either increase the room share or use occupants only to calculate the per-person amount.

Can couples use this calculator?

Yes. Add the couple as one room with two occupants. The calculator shows the room total and divides it by two to display the amount each partner pays.

Can I use square feet or square metres?

Yes. Select either square feet or square metres. Use the same measurement unit for every room.

What is the custom room adjustment?

The custom room adjustment lets you lower or raise one room's weighted value when there is an important difference not covered by the standard options.

What if all bedrooms are the same size?

If all rooms are the same size and have the same features, the unequal portion will be divided evenly. Different occupants or selected features can still change the final amounts.

Can this calculator handle multiple roommates and couples?

Yes. Add one row for each room or roommate group and enter the correct number of occupants for each.

Does this calculator include utilities?

No. It calculates monthly rent only. Utilities are usually divided separately by person, usage, or another household agreement.

Do the calculated room totals add up to the full rent?

Yes. The calculator distributes the complete monthly rent across all entered rooms, subject only to minor rounding in the displayed values.

Is the calculated rent split legally binding?

No. The result is an estimate for roommate discussions. Everyone should agree to the final arrangement and follow the lease and applicable rental rules.
