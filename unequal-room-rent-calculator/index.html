About the Unequal Room Rent Calculator

The WellSplit Unequal Room Rent Calculator helps roommates divide rent when bedrooms are different sizes. It is useful for apartments, houses, student rentals, and shared homes where an equal rent split does not reflect the value of each person's private bedroom.

The calculator uses a blended method. One portion of the rent is divided equally among bedrooms, while the remaining portion is divided according to bedroom size. This approach recognizes that all roommates share the home while also accounting for differences in private space.

How to calculate rent for unequal bedrooms

  • Enter the total monthly rent and choose a currency.
  • Select square feet or square metres for bedroom measurements.
  • Choose how much of the rent should be divided equally.
  • Add each bedroom, its size, and its number of occupants.
  • Calculate each bedroom's total and the payment per person.

A higher equal percentage makes room totals more similar. A lower equal percentage gives bedroom size more influence. Roommates can adjust the slider, compare the results, and choose a split everyone considers fair.

Unequal Room Rent Calculator FAQs

Find answers to common questions about splitting rent by bedroom size, sharing a room as a couple, and choosing a fair unequal rent method.

What is an unequal room rent calculator?

An unequal room rent calculator helps roommates divide rent when bedrooms differ in size or value. It assigns more rent to larger or more desirable rooms and less rent to smaller rooms.

How do you split rent when bedrooms are different sizes?

A common method is to divide part of the rent equally and divide the remaining part according to bedroom size. This calculator lets you choose the equal portion and calculates the rest using each room's share of the total bedroom area.

Should rent be divided equally if rooms are different?

Not necessarily. An equal split is simple, but it may feel unfair when one roommate has a much larger bedroom. A blended split combines an equal portion with a room-size-based portion.

How does the equal portion of rent work?

The equal portion is divided evenly among all bedrooms. The remaining unequal portion is divided according to each bedroom's size.

What percentage of rent should be divided equally?

There is no universal rule. Many roommates choose an equal portion between 30% and 50%, then use bedroom size for the rest. The right percentage depends on how much value the household places on shared areas.

How is the unequal portion calculated?

The calculator adds all bedroom sizes, finds each room's percentage of the total bedroom area, and applies that percentage to the unequal portion of the rent.

Can couples use this unequal rent calculator?

Yes. Enter the couple's room as one bedroom and set the occupants to two. The calculator shows the bedroom total and the amount each partner pays.

Does the number of occupants change the room total?

The room total is based on the selected split and bedroom size. Occupants are used to calculate the per-person amount for a shared bedroom.

Can I use square feet or square metres?

Yes. Select square feet or square metres. The calculation works with either unit as long as every bedroom is measured using the same unit.

How do I measure a bedroom?

Measure the room's length and width, then multiply them. For example, a 12-foot by 10-foot room is 120 square feet.

What if a bedroom is not rectangular?

Divide the room into smaller rectangles, calculate each area, and add them together. A reasonable estimate is usually sufficient for a roommate rent discussion.

Can this calculator handle more than three bedrooms?

Yes. You can add multiple bedroom rows and calculate an unequal rent split for a larger shared house or apartment.

What if all bedrooms are the same size?

If all bedrooms are the same size, the unequal portion is divided equally. The final rent split will therefore be equal unless some rooms have more occupants and you compare the per-person amounts.

What if one room is much larger than the others?

The larger room will receive a higher share of the unequal portion. Increasing the unequal percentage makes room-size differences have a stronger effect.

How can roommates agree on a fair unequal rent split?

Start with room measurements, choose an equal-versus-size-based percentage together, review the calculated amounts, and adjust the percentage until everyone considers the result reasonable.

Is a room-size rent split better than an equal split?

It can be fairer when bedrooms are noticeably different. An equal split is easier, but a room-size method better reflects differences in private space.

Does this calculator include utilities?

No. It calculates monthly rent only. Utilities are often divided equally by person or according to actual usage.

Can I copy the calculated rent split?

Yes. After calculating, use the Copy Result button to copy the summary and payment amounts.

Do all room totals add up to the full rent?

Yes. The calculator distributes the complete monthly rent across the entered bedrooms, subject only to normal rounding in the displayed amounts.

Is the calculated result legally binding?

No. The result is an estimate for roommate discussions. Everyone should agree to the final split and follow the lease and applicable rental rules.
