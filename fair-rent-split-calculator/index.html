About the Fair Rent Split Calculator

The WellSplit Fair Rent Split Calculator is a free roommate rent calculator for homes with unequal bedrooms, couples sharing rooms, and different numbers of occupants. It separates monthly rent into a private-bedroom portion and a shared-space portion so the result reflects both room value and common-area use.

The private-bedroom portion is divided according to bedroom size. Larger rooms pay more of this portion and smaller rooms pay less. The shared-space portion represents areas such as the kitchen, living room, hallways, laundry area, and shared bathrooms. You can divide it by person or equally by bedroom.

How to use this roommate rent calculator

  • Enter the total monthly rent and choose a currency.
  • Select square feet or square metres for all bedroom measurements.
  • Choose how much rent represents shared space.
  • Select whether shared-space rent is divided by person or bedroom.
  • Add each bedroom, choose Single or Couple, and enter its size.
  • Calculate each room total and each person’s monthly payment.

This approach is useful for roommates with unequal rooms, couples living with single roommates, multiple couples and singles, and shared houses with different occupancy levels. The result is a practical starting point for a fair household agreement.

Fair Rent Split Calculator FAQs

Find answers about splitting rent by room size, occupants, couples, and shared space.

What is a fair rent split calculator?

A fair rent split calculator helps roommates divide monthly rent using bedroom size and the number of people sharing the home. It provides a clear room total and per-person payment.

How does this rent split calculator work?

Enter the total rent, choose the shared-space percentage, and add each bedroom with its size and occupants. Private-room rent is divided by bedroom size, while shared-space rent is divided by person or equally by bedroom.

How should roommates split rent when rooms are different sizes?

A practical method is to divide the private-bedroom portion in proportion to room size. Larger bedrooms pay more of that portion and smaller bedrooms pay less.

Should rent be split by room or by person?

Private bedroom value is usually divided by room. Shared areas such as the kitchen and living room can be divided by person. This calculator supports both person-based and room-based shared-space splits.

How do couples split rent with single roommates?

Enter the couple as one bedroom group with two occupants. Their private-room share is based on bedroom size, and their shared-space share can account for two people using the common areas.

Does a couple always pay more than a single roommate?

Not always. A couple usually pays more shared-space rent when it is divided by person, but the final amount also depends on bedroom sizes and the chosen shared-space percentage.

What percentage of rent should be assigned to shared space?

There is no universal rule. Many households choose between 30% and 60%, depending on how much of the home is shared and how important the common areas are.

What counts as shared space?

Shared space normally includes the kitchen, living room, dining area, hallways, laundry area, and shared bathrooms.

Can I use square feet or square metres?

Yes. Either unit works because the calculation compares room-size proportions. Use the same measurement unit for every bedroom.

Do I need exact bedroom measurements?

Exact measurements give the most accurate result, but reasonable estimates can still help. Measure length and width and multiply them to find the room area.

How do I calculate bedroom square footage?

For a rectangular room, multiply length by width. A 12-foot by 10-foot bedroom is 120 square feet.

How is the private-room portion calculated?

The calculator adds all bedroom sizes, finds each room’s percentage of the total bedroom area, and assigns the same percentage of private-room rent.

How is shared-space rent calculated?

When divided by person, each room group pays according to its occupants. When divided equally by bedroom, every bedroom group pays the same shared-space amount.

What if two people share one bedroom?

Choose Couple for that bedroom. The calculator treats the room as two occupants and shows both the group total and the amount per person.

Can this calculator handle multiple couples and singles?

Yes. Add one bedroom for each couple or single roommate. The calculator can handle several couples and singles in the same home.

Can I use it for three or more roommates?

Yes. Add the number of bedrooms needed and enter the correct occupancy type and room size for each one.

What if all bedrooms are the same size?

The private-room portion will be equal across bedrooms. The shared-space method and number of occupants may still create different final totals.

What if one roommate has the largest bedroom?

The largest bedroom pays the largest share of private-room rent. The difference depends on room size and the percentage assigned to private space.

Does this calculator include utilities?

No. It calculates rent only. Utilities can be split separately by person, usage, or another household agreement.

Is the calculated rent split legally binding?

No. The result is an estimate for discussion. Roommates should agree to the final split and follow their lease and local rental rules.
