About the Basement / Private Floor Rent Split Calculator

This calculator helps roommates account for a basement, upper floor, or other mostly private living area when dividing monthly rent.

Each private area receives a base value from its size and optional share of common space. A private floor, separate entrance, and private bathroom can then increase that area's effective value.

How to use it

  • Enter the total rent and measurement unit.
  • Choose whether shared space should be included.
  • Set simple privacy premiums.
  • Add each bedroom, basement, or private floor.
  • Enter the size and number of occupants.
  • Select the features each area includes.

Use lower premiums when a basement has disadvantages such as limited light, low ceilings, noise, or reduced comfort.

Basement / Private Floor Rent Split FAQs

Helpful answers about private floors, basements, and roommate rent.

What is a basement or private floor rent calculator?

It helps roommates divide rent when one person or group has a basement suite, private floor, or similarly separate area.

How does the calculator work?

Each space receives a base value from its size. Private-floor features then increase that space's effective value before rent is divided.

What counts as a private floor?

A private floor is an area mainly used by one roommate or group, such as a basement level, upper floor, or self-contained suite.

How much more should a private floor cost?

There is no fixed amount. A premium of 10% to 30% may be reasonable depending on privacy, size, layout, and included features.

Should a basement always cost more?

Not always. A basement may have more space but less natural light or lower ceilings. Use a lower premium when the space has drawbacks.

What if the basement has a separate entrance?

A separate entrance can justify an extra premium because it adds privacy and independence.

What if the private floor has its own bathroom?

A private bathroom can increase the value of that space and may justify an additional premium.

What if the basement has both a private entrance and bathroom?

Select both options. The calculator applies both adjustments to that space.

Can room size also affect rent?

Yes. The base split uses the size of each private area before applying any private-floor features.

Can couples use this calculator?

Yes. Enter the couple as one space with two occupants. The result will show both the group total and per-person amount.

Can multiple people share one floor?

Yes. Enter the correct number of occupants for that space.

Can I use square feet or square metres?

Yes. Use the same unit for every room, floor, or basement area.

What if one person has a full basement suite?

Enter the entire private area assigned to that person or group, then select any private entrance or bathroom features.

What if common areas are shared?

The calculator can include shared space and divide it equally by room or according to occupants.

What if the basement has poor lighting?

Use a lower private-floor premium to reflect reduced quality or comfort.

What if the private floor has a kitchenette?

A kitchenette can add value. You can reflect it by slightly increasing the private-floor premium.

Does this include utilities?

No. It calculates rent only. Utilities can be divided separately.

Do all totals add up to the full rent?

Yes. The full monthly rent is distributed across all entered spaces, apart from minor display rounding.

Can I copy the result?

Yes. Use the Copy Result button after calculating.

Is the result legally binding?

No. It is an estimate for roommate discussions. Everyone should agree to the final arrangement.
