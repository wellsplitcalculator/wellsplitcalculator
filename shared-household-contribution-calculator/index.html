About the Shared Household Contribution Calculator

The WellSplit Shared Household Contribution Calculator helps two people create a clear monthly contribution plan for shared household expenses and savings.

The calculator supports an equal 50/50 method and an income-based method. Under the income-based method, each person contributes according to their percentage of combined household income.

What can be included

  • Rent or mortgage payments.
  • Utilities and groceries.
  • Shared transportation and insurance.
  • Other agreed household costs.
  • A shared monthly savings goal.

The result shows each person's recommended contribution, contribution percentage, contribution burden, and estimated income remaining afterward. This helps couples and households compare affordability while keeping the full household budget funded.

Shared Household Contribution FAQs

Quick answers about dividing shared household costs and savings.

What is a shared household contribution calculator?

It calculates how much each person should contribute toward shared household costs based on income or an equal split.

How does the calculator work?

Enter each person's monthly income, shared expenses, and optional savings goal. The calculator assigns a recommended contribution.

Can couples use this calculator?

Yes. It is designed for couples and other two-person households.

Can roommates use this calculator?

Yes. Any two people sharing household costs can use it.

What expenses should be included?

Include shared housing, utilities, groceries, transportation, insurance, and other agreed household costs.

Can shared savings be included?

Yes. Add a monthly shared savings goal to include it in the recommended contributions.

What is an income-based contribution?

Each person contributes according to their percentage of combined household income.

What is an equal contribution?

Each person contributes half of the total shared household amount.

Which method is fairer?

Income-based contributions can feel fairer when incomes differ, while equal contributions are simpler.

Should I use take-home income?

Take-home income is usually best because it reflects money actually available.

Can I use gross income?

Yes, but both people should use the same income type.

What if both incomes are equal?

Income-based and equal contributions will produce the same result.

What if one person has no income?

The income-based method assigns them no contribution unless the household agrees otherwise.

Does the calculator show money left over?

Yes. It shows estimated income remaining after the recommended contribution.

What is contribution burden?

It is the percentage of each person's income used for shared household costs.

Can personal expenses be included?

No. Only include expenses shared by the household.

Do the contributions cover the full total?

Yes. Recommended contributions add up to all shared expenses and savings entered.

Can I change the method later?

Yes. Switch between income-based and equal splitting at any time.

Can I copy the result?

Yes. Use the Copy Result button after calculating.

Is the result legally binding?

No. It is a planning tool for household discussions.
