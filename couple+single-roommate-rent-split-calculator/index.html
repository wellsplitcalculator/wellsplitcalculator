About the Couple + Single Roommate Rent Split Calculator

The WellSplit Couple + Single Roommate Rent Split Calculator helps three-person households divide rent when two partners share one bedroom and a single roommate occupies another. It accounts for both private bedroom size and each group's use of shared areas.

The calculator separates monthly rent into a private-room portion and a shared-space portion. The private-room amount is divided according to bedroom size. The shared-space amount can be divided by person, split equally between the couple and the single roommate, or assigned using a one-third couple share.

How to use this couple and roommate rent calculator

  • Enter the total monthly rent and choose a currency.
  • Select square feet or square metres for both room measurements.
  • Choose the percentage of rent assigned to shared space.
  • Enter the couple's bedroom size and the single roommate's bedroom size.
  • Select how the shared-space portion should be divided.
  • Calculate the couple's total, each partner's payment, and the single roommate's rent.

This method is useful when an equal three-way split would ignore bedroom differences or when the household wants the couple's common-area usage reflected in the result. The calculation provides a clear starting point, but everyone should review and agree to the final rent arrangement.

Couple + Single Roommate Rent Split FAQs

These answers cover common questions about splitting rent between a couple sharing one room and a single roommate.

How should a couple and one roommate split rent?

A practical method is to separate rent into a private-room portion and a shared-space portion. The private-room portion is divided by bedroom size, while the shared-space portion is divided using the method the household agrees on.

Should a couple pay more rent than a single roommate?

Usually, yes, because two people use the kitchen, living room, bathroom, and other common areas. However, the exact amount should also reflect bedroom size and the chosen shared-space split.

How does this couple and roommate rent calculator work?

Enter the total rent, both bedroom sizes, the shared-space percentage, and the preferred shared-space split. The calculator then shows the couple's total, each partner's share, and the single roommate's payment.

What is the private-room portion of rent?

The private-room portion represents the value of the bedrooms. It is divided according to the size of the couple's room and the single roommate's room.

What is the shared-space portion of rent?

The shared-space portion represents the kitchen, living room, hallways, laundry area, and shared bathrooms. It can be divided by person or according to another agreed ratio.

What percentage of rent should be assigned to shared space?

There is no universal rule. Many households choose 30% to 60%, depending on how much of the home consists of common areas.

What does 'by person: couple pays 2/3' mean?

It means the couple is treated as two people and the single roommate as one person. The couple pays two-thirds of the shared-space rent, while the single roommate pays one-third.

What does 'couple pays half' mean?

It means the shared-space rent is split equally between the couple as one group and the single roommate as the other group.

What does 'couple pays 1/3' mean?

It means the couple pays one-third of the shared-space rent and the single roommate pays the remaining two-thirds.

When would a couple pay only one-third of shared-space rent?

That option may be used when the single roommate receives more common-area value or when the household has a special agreement. It is less common than splitting by person or equally by group.

How is the couple's private-room share calculated?

The calculator compares the couple's bedroom size with the total size of both bedrooms and assigns the same proportion of the private-room rent.

How is the single roommate's private-room share calculated?

The single roommate's share is based on that bedroom's percentage of the combined bedroom area.

How is the couple's total divided between partners?

The calculator divides the couple's total equally between both partners.

Can I use square feet or square metres?

Yes. Select square feet or square metres. Use the same measurement unit for both bedrooms.

How do I measure a bedroom?

Measure the room's length and width, then multiply them. For example, a 12-foot by 10-foot room is 120 square feet.

What if both bedrooms are the same size?

The private-room portion will be divided equally. The selected shared-space method will determine whether the final totals are equal or different.

What if the couple has the larger bedroom?

The couple will pay a larger share of the private-room portion because their bedroom represents more of the total private space.

Does this calculator include utilities?

No. It calculates monthly rent only. Utilities can be divided separately, often equally by person or according to usage.

Do the calculated payments add up to the full rent?

Yes. The couple's total and the single roommate's total together equal the full monthly rent, subject only to minor display rounding.

Is this rent split legally binding?

No. The result is an estimate for household discussions. Everyone should agree to the final split and follow the lease and applicable rental rules.
