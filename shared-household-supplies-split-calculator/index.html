About the Shared Household Supplies Split Calculator

Household supplies like cleaning products, paper goods, and toiletries can add up quickly. This calculator helps households split these costs fairly, accounting for different usage patterns and product preferences.

How to use this supply splitter

  • Add each household member and their usage factor.
  • Add supply items with prices, categories, and who uses them.
  • Choose your split method (by item, usage, equal, or custom).
  • Add tax and delivery fee if applicable.
  • Calculate to see each person's fair share.

Shared Household Supplies FAQs

Common questions about splitting household supply costs.

What counts as household supplies?

Household supplies include cleaning products (all-purpose cleaner, disinfectant, glass cleaner), paper goods (toilet paper, paper towels, tissues), laundry supplies (detergent, dryer sheets, stain remover), kitchen supplies (dish soap, sponges, garbage bags), bathroom supplies (soap, shampoo, conditioner, toothpaste), and other household essentials.

Why split supplies by item?

Item-level tracking ensures everyone pays only for what they use. This is especially useful when people have different product preferences (e.g., organic vs. conventional) or when some items are only used by specific individuals.

What is a usage factor?

Usage factor adjusts for different consumption habits. A factor of 1.2 means someone uses 20% more supplies than average. This is useful when one person uses more cleaning products, toiletries, or paper goods than others.

How do we handle shared items like toilet paper?

Mark shared items as "Shared" in the assignment dropdown. They'll be split according to your chosen method (by usage, equally, or by item tracking).

What about personal toiletries?

Personal toiletries like shampoo, conditioner, and body wash can be assigned to specific individuals. They'll only pay for what they use.

Should we include tax and delivery fees?

Yes, tax and delivery fees are part of the total cost and should be split fairly. The calculator adds them to the total before splitting.

What if someone uses eco-friendly or premium products?

Use item-level tracking to assign premium items to the person who chooses them. This ensures that only those who benefit from premium products pay for them.

Can this calculator handle more than 2 people?

Yes! You can add as many people as needed using the "Add Person" button. Great for shared houses with multiple roommates.

How do we handle bulk purchases?

For bulk items shared over multiple months, you can split the cost when you buy it. The calculator treats it as a single purchase — you can note the estimated usage period in the item description.

What about different product preferences?

Item-level tracking is perfect for this. Each person can add their preferred products, and the calculator will assign costs accordingly.

Should couples split supplies equally?

Not necessarily. If one person uses significantly more supplies or has specific product preferences, a different split method may be fairer. The calculator lets you compare different methods.

What if we take turns buying supplies?

If you take turns, you might not need a split calculator. But if you want to track fairness over time, you can use the calculator to see if the costs balance out over multiple shopping trips.

How often should we split supplies?

Most households split after each shopping trip or monthly. The calculator works for any period — just enter the items from your most recent purchase.

What about supplies for guests?

Guest supplies like extra toilet paper or hand soap can be treated as shared items. The calculator's "Shared" assignment ensures everyone contributes fairly.

Should we include cleaning supplies separately?

Cleaning supplies are included in the calculator with their own category. They can be split equally, by usage, or assigned to specific people based on who does the cleaning.

What if someone is allergic to certain products?

Use item-level tracking to assign special products to specific individuals. This ensures that only those who need special products pay for them, while shared items like basic cleaning supplies are split fairly.

Can this calculator handle subscription deliveries?

Yes, you can add subscription items (like monthly soap or detergent deliveries) as regular items. The calculator works for one-time and recurring purchases.

How do we handle items that last multiple months?

You can split the cost when purchased and track usage over time. Some households keep a running tally and adjust the split based on actual usage.

Is this calculator free to use?

Yes, completely free. We believe fair household supply splitting should be accessible. The calculator is supported by ads to keep it free.
