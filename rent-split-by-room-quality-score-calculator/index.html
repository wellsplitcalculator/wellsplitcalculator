About the Rent Split by Room Quality Score Calculator

The WellSplit Rent Split by Room Quality Score Calculator helps roommates divide rent when bedroom value depends on more than square footage alone.

Each room receives a score from 1 to 10 based on factors such as privacy, natural light, noise, storage, layout, condition, and overall comfort. The calculator combines that score with room size and optional shared-space allocation.

How to use this calculator

  • Enter the total monthly rent and choose a currency.
  • Select square feet or square metres.
  • Enter shared-space size and choose how it should be divided.
  • Set the importance of room size and room quality.
  • Add each room, its size, occupants, and quality score.
  • Calculate each room total and per-person amount.

This method is useful when rooms differ in sunlight, privacy, noise, storage, shape, condition, or other qualities that are difficult to measure directly.

Room Quality Score Rent Split FAQs

These answers explain how bedroom size, quality scores, shared space, and occupancy can affect a fair roommate rent split.

What is a rent split by room quality score calculator?

It helps roommates divide rent using a simple quality score for each bedroom. Higher-quality rooms pay more, while lower-quality rooms pay less.

How does the calculator work?

Enter the total rent, room size, occupants, shared-space settings, and a quality score for each room. The calculator combines size and quality to determine each room's share.

What does the room quality score mean?

The score represents the room's overall value compared with the others. It can reflect privacy, natural light, noise, layout, condition, storage, and general comfort.

What quality score should I use?

Use a simple 1 to 10 scale. A score of 5 can represent an average room, while lower or higher scores represent below-average or above-average quality.

Should room size and quality both matter?

Yes. Room size measures private space, while the quality score captures differences that square footage alone cannot explain.

How strongly does the quality score affect rent?

The quality importance slider controls its influence. A higher percentage makes quality differences affect the final rent more strongly.

Can I ignore room size?

Set room-size importance to 0% if you want the split to depend only on room quality and shared-space allocation.

Can I ignore room quality?

Set quality importance to 0% if you want the split to depend only on room size and shared space.

What factors can be included in a quality score?

Common factors include sunlight, noise, privacy, closet space, room shape, temperature, floor level, view, condition, and distance from shared areas.

Should a private bathroom be included in the quality score?

Yes. A private bathroom can be reflected by giving that room a higher quality score.

Should parking be included in the quality score?

It can be, but only if the household wants parking value included in the same score. Otherwise, use a separate parking calculator.

Can couples use this calculator?

Yes. Enter the couple's room once and set the occupants to two. The room total will be divided equally between both partners.

Can multiple people share one room?

Yes. Enter the correct number of occupants and the calculator will show the per-person amount.

Can I use square feet or square metres?

Yes. Use either square feet or square metres, but keep the same unit for all rooms and shared-space measurements.

How is shared space handled?

Shared space can be excluded, divided equally by room, or divided according to occupants.

What if all rooms have the same quality score?

Then quality will not create any differences, and the result will depend on room size, shared space, and occupants.

What if all rooms are the same size?

Then the quality scores will determine the differences, subject to the selected quality importance.

Does this calculator include utilities?

No. It calculates monthly rent only. Utilities can be divided separately.

Do all room totals add up to the full rent?

Yes. The full monthly rent is distributed across all rooms, subject only to minor display rounding.

Is the result legally binding?

No. It is an estimate for roommate discussions. Everyone should agree to the final split and follow the lease.
